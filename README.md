
# 🦠 COVID-19 Mortality Analysis — "Covid: A Major Disruptor"

**Tableau · Public Health Analytics**

A Tableau analysis examining how COVID-19 contributed to overall U.S. mortality from 2020 to 2023. It combines a weekly time-series of total vs. COVID deaths, a statistical correlation view, and a state-level choropleth map to quantify the pandemic's true impact on death rates.

## 📊 Key Visualizations

1. **Weekly Total vs. COVID Deaths.** A dual-axis time series (Apr 2020 – Apr 2023) showing how COVID deaths tracked overall mortality wave for wave.
2. **Correlation Scatter.** A statistical view confirming a strong, significant positive relationship between COVID and total deaths (p < .0001).
3. **Deaths-per-1,000 Map.** A continental U.S. choropleth showing COVID mortality burden by state and surfacing geographic hotspots.

## 🗂️ Dataset

U.S. mortality records (total and COVID-attributed deaths) by week and state, 2020–2023. Source: [dataset link].

## 🎯 Project Purpose

The analysis moves past raw case counts to quantify COVID's contribution to excess mortality, giving public-health audiences a clear, evidence-based read on when and where the pandemic hit hardest.

## 👥 Stakeholders

- **Public Health Officials:** Identify the timing and geography of mortality spikes.
- **Epidemiologists & Researchers:** Use the correlation evidence to support excess-mortality findings.
- **Policy Analysts:** Compare state-level burden to inform resource allocation.
- **Data Analysts:** Reference the dual-axis and statistical-modeling techniques.

## 🏆 Achievements and Metrics

- Demonstrated that COVID deaths track total deaths wave for wave (winter 2020–21, Delta late 2021).
- Established a statistically significant correlation (p < .0001) — COVID as a primary driver of excess mortality, not a side effect.
- Mapped clear state-level hotspots in deaths per 1,000 residents.

## 🚀 How to Use

Open the `.twbx` in Tableau (Desktop or Public). Use the time-series and map to explore mortality across periods and states, and hover for per-state and per-week detail.

## ✅ Conclusion

A focused public-health analysis that uses time-series, statistical, and geographic views together to tell a rigorous story about the pandemic's effect on mortality.
